Abstract

Vision-based imitation learning has recently enabled impressive progress in robotic manipulation. However, contact-rich tasks demand force feedback, as vision alone cannot capture intricate contact dynamics. Existing methods pursue two directions: feature-level fusion concatenates visual and force representations within shared backbones, yet suffers from modality collapse where high-dimensional visual features dominate optimization and suppress force signals; policy-level fusion merges separate policy outputs in action space, yet is often phase-agnostic that cannot adapt to contact phase.

We present FCAR, an architecture that addresses both limitations through action-space decoupling and a learnable adaptive gate. FCAR uses a vision-proprioception base policy for nominal motion and a force-conditioned residual for contact correction, fused by a gate that allocates authority based on the interaction state without contact labels. By isolating modalities until the action space, FCAR structurally prevents modality collapse while enabling seamless phase transitions.

Experiments on real-world peg-in-hole insertion, drawer manipulation, and surface wiping show 88.3% average success rate, outperforming the strongest feature-level and policy-level baselines by 11.6%, with strong generalization to unseen physical conditions.

Method

FCAR enforces functional separation by strictly decoupling modalities in the action space: a visual-proprioceptive Base Policy drives global geometric planning, while a force-conditioned Residual Policy provides local, high-frequency corrections. These streams are integrated via a learnable Adaptive Gating Mechanism that autonomously shifts control authority based on the real-time interaction state.

Final action: \( a_t = a^{\text{base}}_t + \alpha_t \cdot \Delta a_t \), where \( a^{\text{base}}_t \) is the vision-driven nominal action, \( \Delta a_t \) the force-conditioned residual, and \( \alpha_t \in [0, 1] \) the learnable gate.

Two-Stage Training

Stage 1: Base policy trained independently on vision-proprioception only. Stage 2: Base frozen; residual policy and adaptive gate trained with stop-gradient on base output and L1 sparsity on gate activation.

Experiments

We evaluate on a 7-DoF Franka Panda with three contact-rich tasks: Peg-in-Hole (PIH), Drawer, and Wiping. Expert demonstrations collected via GELLO teleoperation; 50 demos per task, 20 trials per condition.
